News
  • A deadly week of traffic violence in November saw at least 8 people killed or seriously injured, including two bicyclists. Dominique Jones was killed in Strawberry Mansion and Jason Lohr is in critical condition after being hit on Frankford Ave. Two pedestrians were seriously injured right outside City Hall. Read our statement and post.
     
  • PBA called on City Council to address this horrible string of crashes and commit to increased funding for Vision Zero. So far, only Councilmember O'Rouke has issued a statement.
     
  • Over the summer, Council President Johnson told reporters he supported an additional $1 million for Vision Zero. Contact his office this week to express your appreciation for his supportive statements.
     
  • üìù Sign our new petition for Spruce & Pine! We launched a new petition, in collaboration with 5th Square, to demonstrate that far more people support the plan for concrete curb separation on Spruce and Pine St than oppose it. More than 1,000 people have already signed!
     
  • Spruce & Pine St Recap: Read our post for details and next steps for the City's Spruce and Pine St Safety Project and how it will benefit drivers, pedestrians, and micro-mobility users.
     
  • A representative of PBA will be participating in the City's monthly stakeholder meetings for the Spruce and Pine St Safety Project.
     
  • The 'Get Out The Bike Lane' bill will not be enforced on Spruce and Pine St until the new Loading Zones are implemented in Spring 2025, according to OTIS. The bill goes into effect immediately where signs already say No Stopping. CBS reports.
     
  • We hand-delivered 500 signatures supporting a bike lane on 47th St, to Councilmember Jamie Gauthier at a public meeting detailing the 47th St. Traffic Calming project. Our petition included 253 of those signatures live within 1 mile of 47th St.
     
  • We marched with community groups, activists, and fellow Philadelphians at the 2024 Peoplehood Parade where we carried amazing hand-made bicycling animal puppets, along side Neighborhood Bike Works, calling for safer streets.
     
  • We tabled at Cranksgiving 2024, a scavenger hunt-style food drive food drive that collected over 6,000 lbs of food by bicycle for Philabundance! Read more at The Trellis.
     
  • PBA is now on Bluesky! Give us a follow to keep up with new events and the latest Philly bike happenings! 
     
  • The City has released new guidelines for Temporary Bike Access Routes within Construction Areas. This guide provides regulations for installing temporary bike infrastructure in construction work areas. Please report any construction related issues affecting bike lanes to Philly311!
     
  • Parking-Protected Bike Lane Legislation unfortunately did not pass the PA State Legislature before the end of the year; however, the 2024 saw the closest we‚Äôve gotten to see it come to fruition! PBA will continue to support the Bicycle Coalition in their work to legalize parking-protected bike infrastructure and pedestrian plazas across the Commonwealth.
