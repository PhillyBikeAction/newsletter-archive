Two months left to donate!

Black Girl Joy Bike Ride is an organization working to foster ridership amongst Black Women, one of the most underserved and overlooked demographics of cyclists in our city. Their bi-weekly bike rides make use of Philadelphia’s Indego bikes, significantly reducing the barrier to participation.

The founder of BGJR, Iresha Picot, has teamed up with Philly Bike Action to raise money to increase bike ownership in her community!

What can you do? Donate to help Black Girl Joy Bike Ride expand equity in Philadelphia’s bike scene and reach more Black Women.

News
  • We are deeply saddened by the passing of Jason Lohr, beloved member of the Standard Tap family, who has died from injuries sustained from a collision with a turning car while riding his bike on Frankford Ave in November. (CBS)
     
  • 📝 Sign our petition for Spruce & Pine! We launched a petition, in collaboration with 5th Square, to show that the plan for a concrete curb separated bike lane is extremely popular.
     
  • Bike Lane Cleanup: PBA volunteers braved the cold for Society Hill's Annual Clean Up. Society Hill Civic gave a "special shout out to the volunteers from Philly Bike Action, who showed up early in their pink vests, bringing coffee and donuts, and worked alongside residents to collect bags and bags of leaves."

    Despite Society Hill's opposition to the Spruce/Pine project, we will continue to build bridges with our neighbors.
     
  • Philadelphia Crosstown Coalition of over 20 RCOs voted in support of street safety recommendations that include concrete separated bike lanes, support for the 'Get Out the Bike Lane' bill, and improving visibility at intersections with daylighting infrastructure!
     
  • The 'Get Out The Bike Lane' bill was ceremoniously signed by Mayor Parker in a press conference attended by Philly Bike Action, BCGP, and 5th Square advocates. Parker reiterated that "this is just the first step" with more to come - let's hold her accountable to that promise.
     
  • Watch Council President Kenyatta Johnson call for a round of applause for Philly Bike Action's successful campaign to withdraw the bike lane parking permits.
     
  • Our video about 'Brad the Pit' on Baltimore Ave got the attention of City and State reps!
