Community Meetings for Bike Lane Protection on Spruce & Pine Start in October
 


Photo(s) by ZED Images.

The City of Philadelphia's Office of Transportation and Infrastructure Systems (OTIS) will be presenting plans for protecting the Spruce and Pine bike lanes to community groups along the corridor throughout the month of October.

News
  • Philly Bike Action held a ride and rally to Take the Lane to City Hall and flooded the public comment section of City Council's return to session with support for safer bike infrastructure and increased funding for Vision Zero. Spotlight on America reports.
     
  • We met with Council President Kenyatta Johnson and staff to discuss what City Council can do to address the demands of 6,000 petition signers asking for safer streets.
     
  • No Stopping in the Bike Lane Bill was introduced on behalf of Council President Johnson. This bill will prohibit parking in bike lanes citywide where many (including Spruce and Pine) currently allow for 25 minutes of parking. This legislation is a critical first step in protecting our bike lanes with infrastructure. 
     
  • City Council also resolved to hold a hearing examining the City's progress on Vision Zero goals and the safety of cyclists and pedestrians in Philadelphia. This hearing will be held sometime this fall and Philly Bike Action will be turning out public comment - stay tuned!
     
  • Market St Detour campaign wrap: Since March, we've been sharing feedback with PennDOT about improving the bicycle detour on Walnut St Bridge and 23rd St. Read our campaign page to learn about our three big wins!
     
  • Park(ing) Day Winners! Philly Bike Action received the Community Golden Cone Award at Park(ing) Day 2024! We took over a parking space near City Hall to create an interactive art installation of abandoned flex posts.
     
  • 3rd Street Bike Lane: Two weeks ago Councilman Mark Squilla introduced a bill to remove a travel lane and add a bike lane to South 3rd Street running from Market to South Streets. PBA successfully lobbied directly to the Councilman and helped gather community support resulting in a faster than normal process. 3rd street has now been milled and is set to be repaved/re-striped in the coming month(s). This happened way ahead of the initial repaving schedule, but OTIS has been spread thin working on protection for Spruce/Pine.
    Action:  Email mark.squilla@phila.gov and cc anne.kelly@phila.gov with the sample text:  "I understand that 3rd street is being re-striped soon. Can you ensure that a bike lane is painted when this happens?" 
     
  • Bike safety on Vine Street: PBA attended a virtual meeting led by PennDOT regarding the Vine Street Corridor Safety Improvement project. A Q&A session was held after the presentation, featuring heavy input from Philadelphia's biking community on how to improve the proposed bike infrastructure. We encourage you to attend the next in-person public meeting on October 24 (details below).
