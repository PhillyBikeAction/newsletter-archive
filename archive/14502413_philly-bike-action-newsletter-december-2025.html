December 2025 Newsletter

Members have elected our 2026 Board of Directors!

In reaching this important milestone, we truly are Philadelphia’s only all-volunteer, democratically-run bicycle advocacy group. We appreciate every candidate who ran for the Board in this election, and every volunteer who helped administer it. We depend on our volunteers for everything we do—including Board service—and gratitude goes to everyone who stepped up to do this important job.

Our organization has accomplished far more for safer, livable streets than anyone thought possible—but there’s so much more to do. As PBA enters this new chapter, we look forward to a new year of bike advocacy and everything we will accomplish in 2026.

News

  • We marched in West Philly's Peoplehood Parade again this year! We carried a handmade message for a safer Fairmount Park. Don't miss the pictures!
  • CITYWIDE SOCIAL HOUR TONIGHT 🪩 Instead of our typical meeting, we are gathering at Barcade (Center City) for a fun social hour. Games, food, and drinks available for purchase. Please RSVP for last minute venue change notification.
  • Construction of the City Hall bike lane is underway! 🚧 Flex-posts are being installed along the entire south and east side of Penn Square - a huge improvement! Unfortunately, the north side has a conflict-zone bike lane, because PennDOT, who designed and paid for the project, would not remove the car slip lane citing traffic volumes.
  • ✅ LeCount and Bambrey St will be permanently reversed thanks to Council President Johnson who has introduced legislation to codify the direction change that was implemented after a string of bike lane crashes demanded action.
  • Support a new protected rail-trail corridor between Parkside Ave (Fairmount Park) and City Ave (Bala Cynwyd) at this public meeting on December 3 regarding the Parkside Wynnefield Cynwyd Trail.
  • Do you live in Queen Village, Society Hill, Bella Vista, & Washington Sq West? Meet your elected officials at a public community event on Dec 3. This is an important opportunity to vocalize support for protected bike lanes!
  • PBA linked up with Cobbs Creek Ambassadors for one of their clean-ups along the Cobbs Creek Trail and were joined by Council Member Jamie Gauthier! The fall foliage was incredible - a great place for a bike ride! 🍂
