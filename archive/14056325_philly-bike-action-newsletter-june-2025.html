Bike Lanes for All - Postcards to City Council

Send your personal message on a postcard to City Council demanding protected bike lanes for ALL of Spruce and Pine - no carve outs, no delay! 

We need protected bike lanes river to river and we can't wait another year. Remind City Council we want concrete now!

Fill out your postcard in-person at the June Citywide (tomorrow! see below) or District meetings throughout June. Don't worry about postage; we will deliver them.

News

  • 🚨 Save SEPTA! 🚨 Sign this petition from Transit for All PA to send letters to Governor Shapiro and your state representatives asking them to fund transit, preventing service cuts and fare increases. Follow for updates and plan to attend one of the events and rallies hosted by Transit for All PA.
  • Exciting news! the new 47th St bike lane was approved last week, thanks to leadership from Councilmember Jamie Gauthier and everyone who signed our petition! (Read more in Philly Voice)

    AND OTIS announced that the 3rd Street Bike Lane Project, which PBA tirelessly advocated for, will soon come to fruition this summer! 

    The success of these projects demonstrates the importance of showing up to express support for things people want to see in their neighborhood.
  • Loading Zone and No Stopping enforcement FINALLY coming to Spruce and Pine! Signs are going up along the corridor to educate drivers!
  • We'll have a table at Pride on Passyunk on June 8! Stop by our table to share your thought on safe streets and grab a special 🌈 PBA Pride sticker!
  • We teamed up with CCRA to clean the Spruce Street bike lane; beautifying the area and making the lane safer and more pleasant for everyone using it!
  • We also tackled Spring Garden Bridge, a vital connector between West Philly and Center City. We collected over 40 bags full of trash! See our Instagram post here for more details.
  •  The Schuylkill River Trail just got bigger! This new section connects the SRT to Christian St, giving South Philly a new path for trails and green space.
  • PBA tabled at Girard Ave Street Festival where we spoke with festival goers about the benefits of daylighting and parking-protected bike lanes. We received tons of interest from residents for making their neighborhood safer!
  • Vision Zero Budget: we testified at City Hall in support of the Mayor's proposed $5 million for Vision Zero funding and money for Spruce and Pine. Watch this excellent public comment from a supporter!
  • CHOP is planning a 1,000 car parking garage in Grays Ferry. The driveway will endanger people in the bike lane. Here's a petition to oppose the project.
  • We updated our website! See all the ways you can get involved!
