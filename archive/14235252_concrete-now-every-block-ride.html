Ride with us September 5:

Join us Friday, September 5th on the Art Museum Steps for a brief rally followed by a short ride to City Hall. We demand a safety plan that protects every block of the Spruce and Pine bike lanes. Scroll down for additional actions.

Date: Friday September 5

Tell us you are going

Feel free to bring signs for your bike or show up early to make one with our supplies. Indego stations are available near the start and end of the ride. The Art Museum is easily accessible via the Schuylkill River Trail. For updates, RSVP.

What is going on?

Last year, we took to the streets to demand action in the aftermath of the preventable deaths of Dr. Barbara Friedes and Christopher Cabrera. We rode 500 strong to demand life-saving concrete protected bike lanes.

Since then, Mayor Parker promised to protect the Spruce and Pine bike lanes and included concrete protection in her budget proposal. But that promise is in jeopardy.

The anti-bike lane opposition group suing the city released an internal city memo that revealed a preliminary recommendation to install concrete curb protection on ONLY HALF of the Spruce and Pine bike lanes—compromising everyone’s safety for the convenience of a vocal minority.

Tell City leaders to finish the job. No half measures.

Paint is not protection. Concrete now, every block.
