Report Mobility Obstructions with Laser Vision!

Philly Bike Action has launched a new tool that makes it easy to report dangerous parking that illegally blocks sidewalks, crosswalks, handicap curb cuts, and bike lanes directly to the PPA's Mobility Access Violation form. 

Add the app to your home screen, snap a photo, confirm the automatically detected information, and send! Watch a demo.

Over 650 Postcards to City Council

Over 650 of you have written postcards to City Council with messages about continuous concrete curb protection on Spruce and Pine. 

We hand-delivered these to Council Member Squilla and will deliver the rest to Council President Johnson soon!

Read the postcards on our website!

 

Black Girl Joy Bike Ride Fundraiser Delivers!

Thanks to your generosity during our winter fundraiser with Black Girl Joy Bike Ride, seven Black women started the summer with their first bikes!

We are extremely grateful for the opportunity to work in partnership with founder, Iresha Picot, on her mission to make biking more accessible.

Check out the recent segment on CBS.

News

  • Elections for Philly Bike Action’s Board of Directors will take place in November. Anyone interested in helping to lead our organization- or who wants to find out more about what the Board does- should sign up for the first informational session on July 30. Fill out this form for details!
  • PBA sent a statement demanding safer bike lanes on Spring Garden St after police hit a 68 year old man riding in this bike lane. 
  • Join us July 12 for a bike lane clean up at 50th & Walnut. Details below!
  • Our Finances are now explained on our website - we are 100% powered by small donations. No grants, no staff; just small donors and volunteers!
  • PBA tabled at multiple festivals this month: 🎨 We displayed our art at BADfest in Brewerytown, engaged neighbors at the Baltimore Ave Dollar Stroll, and polled the audience at Summer  Solstice on 4th St ☀️, getting consensus about bike infrastructure in Queen Village.

A summer of huge wins and milestones!

  • In 2023, PBA set a goal to change the signs on Spruce and Pine bike lane to No Stopping - now they are finally installed and enforcement has begun!
     
  • PBA launched a last minute petition for a City Hall bike lane. Over 1000 Philadelphians signed on and Councilmember Young introduced the required legislation just in time! Read more at Philly Voice.
     
  • The final budget was passed and funding for Vision Zero has doubled! Read a detailed breakdown of the budget by The Bicycle Coalition.
