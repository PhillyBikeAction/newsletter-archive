West Market St Petition! Spruce Pine update!

February 2026 Newsletter

Sign the Petition!

Last November, in the span of just 7 days, Meaza Brown and Rosa Mar Espinosa Rodas were crossing West Market St. when they were struck and killed by speeding drivers. 
These horrible fatalities add to the more than 139 documented crashes between 38th St. and the Schuylkill River between 2020 and 2024, 35% of which involved Pedestrians and cyclists. The corridor has been on the high-injury network for years, but the tens of thousands who walk and bike here every day have seen close to no safety improvements.

Sign our petition and join us in demanding that city and state officials implement life-saving infrastructure improvements NOW before tragedy strikes again!

 

What's going on with Spruce and Pine lawsuit?

It has been 1.5 years since a reckless driver killed Dr. Barbara Friedes while she was biking in the Spruce Street bike lane. Why is there still no concrete protection on Spruce and Pine Street?

Despite extensive community support and political promises, a myopic lawsuit has seriously delayed safety upgrades.

Read (and share!) our latest blog post that recaps everything you need to know about the lawsuit, what the holdup is, and what you can do:

Read all about it

The opposition's strategy has been to move the conversation about protected bike lanes into a venue where the public can't participate and delay as possible hoping that we will all move on. We will not forget. Don't let City Council forget either.
