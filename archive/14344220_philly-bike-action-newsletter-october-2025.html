Safer Fairmount Park! 
Belmont "Speedway" Family Picnic - Oct 12

Last month, PBA member Harry Fenton was killed by a speeding driver while riding his bike at in the center of Fairmount Park, at Belmont Ave and Avenue of the Republic.

In response, we launched a petition demanding a safer Fairmount Park, co-signed by a coalition of 9 other groups, which already has over 1,000 signatures.

Now, EVERYONE is invited to PBA's inaugural "Belmont Speedway Family Picnic" in Fairmount Park on October 12! Much like the beloved bike lane parties of yore, we will call attention to unsafe road conditions while having a family-friendly good time. We'll play with speed radars while enjoying music and food. Volunteer form!

Bring a picnic and blanket and a love for safe streets! 

📅 Sunday October 12 (Rain date Oct 19)
⏲️ Time: 12:00 - 2:00 pm
🌳 Location: Belmont Ave in West Fairmount Park (exact location)

News

  • Our second protest ride for safe streets was HUGE. Hundreds of you turned out to show City Hall that we are not going anywhere until our streets are safe to walk and bike. The sudden loss of fellow advocate Harry Fenton to traffic violence just days before prove that we can't wait for protected bike infrastructure. Watch the live rally and view photos from the event. Coverage in CBS, NBC10, and Billy Penn.
  • Spruce and Pine: Due to the lawsuit against the City, the long awaited (and very popular) Loading Zones have been removed. In a controversial move, Society Hill Civic Association voted to give $25,000 of neighborhood money to pay the legal fees of the anti-bike lane group that filed the lawsuit. 🎩
  • We turned out 37 supporters of safe streets to comment on multiple bills at City Council's Streets & Services Committee including Council President Kenyatta Johnson's bill authorizing the City to install Loading Zones (in response to the lawsuit) which was passed out of Committee! (KYWRadio)
  • Despite support from Mayor Parker, multiple RCOs, 1400 petition signers, and 22 public commenters, the City Hall bike lane bill was HELD without explanation by Councilmember Jeffery Young on Monday after he introduced it in June. PennDot has already begun milling the road for the bike lane (Billy Penn). Young needs to pass the bill to legalize it. 
  • ‼️ PBA Board of Directors Election will take place November 14–21. You must be a member to vote. The deadline to become a member for this election is October 15. The easiest way to become a member is to set up a recurring monthly donation of $5 or more. See here for information about membership. Nominations will be open October 15 - 31.
  • Philly Bike Action talks safe streets, concrete protected bike lanes, and e-scooters live with Jessie Amadio in Studio 2 for WHYY.
  • We're also doing a live panel about safe streets with Billy Penn tomorrow Thursday Oct 2 at 5:30pm. We want you in the audience, please RSVP!
  • Over 30 people joined us for our ride to support businesses on Market Street during the new bike lane construction. We snapped great photos of 9 local businesses who support protected bike lanes!
  • We celebrated the new 47th Street bike lane with a ride through the neighborhood with Councilmember Jamie Gauthier and over 50 riders! 
  • We led 3 bike lane cleanups! We picked up 25 trash bags on the 33rd St bridge, CM Mark Squilla dropped by our E Moyamensing clean-up, and we mucked out the curbs and drains on Pine St in Rittenhouse!
  • We supported the Castor Avenue Project by hosting a postcard writing event at the Northeast Regional Library. Over 50 postcards were filled! The project will increase accessibility for everyone, including people on bikes.
  • Open Streets is coming to Queen Village this fall! 4th Street will transform into a vibrant pedestrian paradise with pop-ups and light entertainment on Saturday October 11 & 18 from 11am–5pm. Event info.
  • Save SEPTA bike ride! Wednesday Night Rides and Transit Forward Philadelphia are teaming up for a bike ride advocating for SEPTA funding! October 15th, 7:00pm, 48th and Market Street
