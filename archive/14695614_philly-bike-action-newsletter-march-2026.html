Pop-up "concrete" on Spruce & Pine!

March 2026 Newsletter

 

This Spring: Spruce & Pine Pop-Up Protection!

It has been 1.5 years since a reckless driver killed Dr. Barbara Friedes while she was biking in the Spruce Street bike lane. Why is there still no concrete protection on Spruce and Pine Street in Center City?

Read our blog post for the full story. Then swing by our first in a series of Pop-Up Protected Bike Lane events to see what "concrete" protection looks like in practice!

Saturday March 21
9:00 - 12:00 p.m. | Corner of Spruce and 12th St

We'll be out there chatting with residents and commuters about all the ways protected bike lanes improve quality of life on the street-- for everyone, not just bikers.

Want to volunteer? Fill out our interest form!

News

  • We're launching our first zine on March 7 at Partners & Sons!
  • Did you know that revenue from red light and speed cameras fund safe street projects? $17 millions in funding was just announced for our region, including money to update all bike lane No Parking Signs to No Stopping and "upgrading existing bike lanes with concrete curb separation". 
    Read our interview with Philly Voice about what this means.
  • ⚠️ The most restrictive E-bike bill in the nation was just passed in New Jersey. It adds new regulations for E-bikes while doing very little to improve safety. Attend next Regional Safety Task Force meeting, focused on e-micromobility and help guide evidence based policy that will truly improve safety for everyone. The meeting will take place on April 1st, 9am to noon and the deadline to register is March 23rd.
  • March 28th - Join PBA and BCGP for a group ride to Teddy Einstein's Ghost Bike dedication. Roll from Clark park at 10 am.
  • The snow fall made biking almost impossible last month. PBA participated in some public shaming of the unplowed bike lanes and shoveled some ourselves. Bike lanes are not dumping grounds for plowed snow!
  • Our Snyder Avenue bike lane cleanup collected several bags of trash and cleared snow and ice from the bike lane in multiple spots with support from Pennsport RCO and Whitman Library.
  • Our petition for a Safer West Market Street already has 1,200+ signatures. Help us spread this to students, employees, and residents of West Philly!
  • Market East needs protected bike lanes! Fill out the City's 1-min survey. Select "Traffic & Transportation" and leave a comment about bike lanes!
  • Our photo-series of We Ride in Philly was featured in a 6-page print spread in Grid Magainze last month! Read it online.
