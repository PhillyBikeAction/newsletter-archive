LeCount & South:
Back-to-back crashes, immediate action!

Multiple back-to-back crashes caused by drivers right-hooking people in the bike lane on South at LeCount forced neighbors to jump into action to make it safer.

Philly Bike Action joined them on the street to protect the bike lane and bring attention to their petition to reverse the direction of LeCount St.

It only took a few PBA cones to extend the bike lane enough to slow turning cars during morning and evening rush hours.

By the end of the week, Council President Johnson responded with new street signs and an emergency direction change.

When we show up for each other, we can get things done. This is the power of community organizing! 💪

We are working with Johnson's office to ensure the necessary legislation is passed for LeCount and nearby Bambrey Streets - and ultimately address unsafe conditions on South St bridge.

See our coverage on Instagram, CBS (here and here), abc6, FOX29, Philly Voice, and the Inquirer, 

 

One year later, Spruce and Pine are still UNPROTECTED.

Last July, a driver killed Dr. Barbara Friedes as she was biking in the Spruce Street unprotected bike lane. One year later, we are still waiting for concrete protection that could prevent a similar crash from happening again (CBS).

Just this week, two cars crashed on Spruce street; one flipped over and the other crossed the bike lane and damaged an Indego station -- proof that concrete protection along the entire corridor is needed just as much as ever.

It's been...

  • One year since Mayor Parker promised to protect the bike lanes after 6,000 Philadelphians signed a petition for safer streets.
  • 10 months since hundreds of people attended a community meeting in overwhelming support for concrete curb protection.
  • Six months of internal "community stakeholder" meetings and six months since the last update on the City's project website.
  • Five months since Mayor Parker promised funding for concrete protection in her FY2026 budget proposal.
  • Four months since dozens of people supported the project at a regulations hearing; detailed in the published summary document.
  • Two months since an obstructionist group slapped the City with a frivolous lawsuit intended to delay the project.
  • One month since we delivered nearly 700 hand-written postcards supporting full protection to decision makers.

Remember: Every day is a great day to email Mayor Parker and your Councilperson to say that you are still waiting for concrete protection.

Laser Vision News

Our app, Laser Vision, which easily submits mobility access parking violations now has 500 users with almost 4,000 submissions. Over half of submissions report cars blocking sidewalks, crosswalks and ADA ramps. 

Explore report data on our interactive heat map.

In an interview with CBS, the PPA has acknowledge that they are receiving the reports and use them to inform their beat officers. 

Read and watch coverage in WHYY, Inquirer, Philly Mag, FOX29 and CBS.
