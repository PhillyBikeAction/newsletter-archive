96% of drivers on Belmont Ave are speeding

In October, we were out with speed radars to tally the high-speed traffic that flew past park space on Belmont Ave where families picnicked in West Fairmount Park.

The speed limit is 35 mph. 96% of drivers were speeding and more than HALF where going a speed that would kill a pedestrian in 85% of crashes..... in a park.

There have been 8 deaths along Belmont Ave and 41 deaths in or around Fairmount Park in the last six years.

Over 60 people attended our Belmont "Speedway" Family Picnic in the hopes that our City leaders will take action to:

➡ Lower the speed limit on ALL roads in the park to 25 mph.

➡ Repair the Park’s crumbling bike and pedestrian paths.

➡ Road diet and quick-build traffic calming on Belmont Ave.

Read and watch coverage of our demonstration at WHYY and CBS.

Sign and share our petition with anyone who wants to use our park system safely!

City passes law to legalize the Spruce and Pine St Loading Zones in response to Lawsuit Injunction

City Council unanimously passed a bill authorizing the Streets Department to install Loading Zones in most of Center City. This bill was introduced by Council President Kenyatta Johnson, supported by Councilmember Squilla, and signed by Mayor Parker to address the lawsuit injunction. This legislation is a big step closer to protected bike lanes on Spruce and Pine. 

Read more in The Inquirer and watch on CBS.

Also congratulations to Chris Gale, Executive Director of the Bicycle Coalition for his recognition from City Council President!
