Protest Ride Friday Sept 5:
Demand CONCRETE protection, EVERY block!

Cat is out of the bag - an internal memo has revealed a preliminary recommendation to install concrete curb protection on ONLY HALF of the Spruce and Pine bike lanes in Center City. (What is concrete curb?)

If Mayor Parker, Council President Johnson, and Councilmember Squilla don't demand protection for every block, fatal crashes on unprotected blocks will happen again.

Last year, hundreds of us took to the streets to demand action. THIS FRIDAY we're riding for CONCRETE NOW, EVERY BLOCK! Details here.

Tell City leaders to finish the job. No half measures.

What about this lawsuit?

A lawsuit against the bike lanes has resulted in a temporary injunction prohibiting implementation of the loading zones on Spruce and Pine street with the ultimate goal of prohibiting concrete protection entirely.

The lawsuit lawyer is George Bochetto (yes that one) and the judge who granted the preliminary injunction was Judge Sierra Thomas Street.

Not only does this injunction make our bike lanes less safe, it denies loading areas for residents, businesses, delivery drivers, and taxis.

City Council can easily resolve this matter. We are calling on them to pass legislation to explicitly authorize the Streets Department to install loading zones where necessary.

Our streets are a public space and their use should not be dictated by a small minority of wealthy individuals who have resources to bully the City with lawsuits.

Keep the pressure on by contacting City Council with our pre-filled emails.

News

  • We hand-delivered 350 postcards to Council President Johnson from constituents who want concrete protection on every block of the Spruce and Pine bike lanes! We collected over 650 postcards in total!
  • PBA was out on Spruce Street with pop-up bike lane protection! We protected 3 blocks with 60 cones to demonstrate how the street would function with a protected bike lane. Spoiler alert: it works great!
  • Elections for Philly Bike Action’s Board of Directors will take place in November. Our FINAL informational session will be on Sept 15. This is the last chance for those interested in helping to lead our organization or learning more about what the Board does. Fill out this form for details!

  • We participated in community events around the city last month, including the Philadelphia Running Alliance's Mayor's Cup, Join Philly's Fishtown Mini Activities Fair, and Council President Kenyatta Johnson's Annual Community Appreciation Day in Point Breeze.
  • Over 20 volunteers pulled weeds and cleaned the bike lane and sidewalks on South 11th St including a vacant lot in cooperation with local businesses and the Hawthorne and Bella Vista RCOs. View the before and after photos!
