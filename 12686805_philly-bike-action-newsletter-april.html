News
Last month was next level - Philly Bike Action is on fire üî• and we want to celebrate our recent successes!
 
  • Our petition to #withdrawthepermits has over 750 signatures!



    We launched an official campaign to withdraw the Spruce-Pine bike lane parking permits. So far, we've sent over 300 comments to OTIS, who we believe has a responsibility to find an alternative arrangement. Our goal is 1000 signatures, at which point we will take the petition to City Council.
  • We packed a meeting with Council Member Mark Squilla. 

    Queen Village Neighbors Association presented a proposal for a 3rd St bike lane and there was overwhelming audience support for doing more to keep bike lanes clear of obstructions. Cyclist turnout at community meetings makes a huge difference!
     
  • Raised over $500 in our first month of accepting donations!

    THANK YOU. Your financial support thus far amounts to over $100/mo recurring, which will begin to pay our room rental fees. Become a supporter for $5/mo to help cover our digital service expenses.
  • Jumped into action advocating for a safe bicycle detour when Market Street Bridge closes for renovation next year. We're talking to PennDOT (PDF) and community stakeholders.
     
  • We reached 1000 followers on Instragram
     
  • We gave a seminar on safe street advocacy at Philly Bike Expo
     
  • Presented to the Fairmount Civic Association
     
  • Tech Team crushing it with custom donation platform and campaign tool. Watch our calendar for upcoming meetings.
