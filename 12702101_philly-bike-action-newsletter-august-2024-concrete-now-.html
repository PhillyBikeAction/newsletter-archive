August 2024 Newsletter
 
Philly Bike Action is a group devoted to improving bike infrastructure for all of Philadelphia. 
 
Every Sunday in August, we NEED you in the buffer.


Tenth Presbyterian uses 6 blocks of bike lanes as weekend parking lots, including the block where Dr. Friedes was killed. As long as these bike lanes are used for parking, we won't be able to protect them with concrete.

Last week, we expanded our demonstration to Pine Street. Civil Affairs Officers removed our cones. In light of Dr. Friedes' death, the writing is on the wall for these permits, but we need all bodies and bikes in the buffer for the next few Sundays to keep our bike lanes open until we reach an agreement with the Church. 

Sign up to join our human bike lane buffer!
News
  • NEW BIG WIN: Old Pine Street Church has WITHDRAWN its bike lane parking permit as of last night! Thank you Old Pine for demonstrating leadership in the community by prioritizing safety over convenience.
     
  • We held a Vigil for Victims of Traffic Violence in response to a deadly night on Philadelphia's streets. Everyone came to pay their respects to Dr. Barbara Friedes who was killed while biking in the Spruce St bike lane and Christopher Cabrera who was killed by a car while standing on the sidewalk. Coverage in the Inquirer, Billy Penn and NBC.
     
  • Hundreds of people joined us for our Safe Streets Now protest ride from the Art Museum to City Hall calling for immediate concrete upgrades to our unprotected bike lanes. Coverage in the Inquirer, Billy Penn, Philly Voice, and more photos.
     
  • A cut is a cut Despite the Mayor's claim that Vision Zero money was merely "redistributed", we're committed to seeing this important budget line fully restored. Share our post with a simple explanation of why Mayor Parker's statement is misleading.
     
  • Attend our Safe Streets Now Town Hall on Aug 5 to learn more about our campaign to get concrete protected bike lanes. This Citywide meeting will dive into our demands and our methods for holding elected officials accountable.
     
  • Church Bike Lane Parking: We can't add concrete protection to our bike lanes if people refuse to stop parking in them. The party is over. From now on, we'll be out there every Sunday, on Spruce and Pine, using our bodies to Protect the Bike Lane until Tenth Presbyterian withdraws the bike lanes from their parking permits. Bring signs. Volunteer sign up here. Please watch our Instagram for behavior guidelines.
     
  • Are you new here? We organize by City Council District. Attend the monthly meeting of the District you live in to learn about how to get involved. Join our Discord. Learn more about us here.
     
  • Make your Philly Bike Action user account Sign up here to connect your donations, event sign-ins, and Discord account in order to unlock more features. This will eventually be mandatory to fully participate on Discord.
