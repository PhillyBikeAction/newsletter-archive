Newsletter, Issue 2

 
Philly Bike Action! is a group devoted to improving bike infrastructure for all of Philadelphia. 
News
  • Mass Email Monday: Thank you everyone who emailed their District Councilperson this past week. We will be continuing this event tomorrow (6/19/23). If you haven't sent an email yet, you can find a template here: https://bikeaction.org/event/mass-email-monday/. If you got a reply, then follow up. If you didn't, then forward the email with something along the lines of "Hey, just wanted to follow up on this." Most importantly, be persistent.
  • Spruce/Pine Bike Lane Parking: No news from Kenyatta Johnson's district but we did get some replies from Councilman Squilla himself. To summarize, I will share this excerpt from the executive director at Bicycle Coalition which is consistent with Councilman Squilla's replies: 
"The history of the City allowing 1) cars to stand for 20 minutes on certain blocks and 2) religious services parking on Spruce & Pine during the weekends dates back many decades. When Mayor Nutter decided to convert a travel lane into a buffered bike lane in 2009, he made an agreement with the residents and the Churches that the practice of motorists using the lane would continue. In fact, the only reason the churches and Center City Residents Association, Washington West Civic Association and Society Hill Civic Association agreed to the conversion was because the practices were allowed to continue. Since 2009, there have been various attempts by different entities, individuals and ourselves to change the practice and protect the lanes to eliminate the standing/parking. The residents and the churches are vehemently opposed. We did make some modest improvements: our advocacy got the lanes partially protected at the intersections and the Parking Authority has stepped up enforcement with a unit of enforcement officers on bikes who dramatically increased the # of tickets written. Our current ask is for the city to create more loading zones so that residents donâ€™t need to stand to unload/load in the bike lane. The City has told us that their sign shop is too backed up and they have no funding to make the signs."
 
Events
  • Mass Email Monday: All day event, Monday, 6/19/23. Reach out to your district councilperson and let them know you want safer bike lanes. This month, we are focused on bike lane parking. A template for the Spruce/Pine bike lane can be found in the link. 
  • Sign Making Workshop: Time and Place TBD, Saturday, 6/24/23. We are making signs for the upcoming Sunday Slow Ride to protest the permitted bike lane parking for churches and other religious houses. Reply if you are interested so we can get a head count. Also, if you have any suggestions on a place to host (need tables and chairs), please reach out. 
  • Philly Bike Action! General Meeting: Schuylkill River Park, 7pm, Monday 7/3/23. This is our 2nd general meeting. We will be meeting at the same place as our meeting. If it rains, we will meet at Lubert Plaza pavilion at the corner of 10th and Locust street next to Thomas Jefferson University Hospital. We will share the agenda on our website and in the next newsletter. 
  • Sunday Slow Ride: Schuylkill River Park, 10am-12pm, Sunday 7/9/23. We will be starting at the Schuylkill River Park and riding in a loop down Pine and Spruce streets. We will use the bike lanes when they are available. Where the lanes are blocked, we will take the lane and ride slowly (about 4 mph) to share our dissatisfaction with the current situation. Safety vests, GoPros, fun music, and signs are highly encouraged. 
Volunteer Opportunities
There are many ways to get involved with Philly Bike Action! These are the roles we are currently trying to fill. If you have a suggestion for something not on this list, feel free to reach out.
  • District Leaders: We are looking for leaders to host monthly meetups and events in their own districts. We are looking for district leaders for all City Council District from 1-10 right now. The goal is to share this responsibility between 2 other people in the same district. 
  • Social media coordinator: Sharing group events and photos on platforms such as Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter
  • Newsletter editor: Gather information, edit, and send out biweekly newsletters. We use Mailchimp as our email platform.
