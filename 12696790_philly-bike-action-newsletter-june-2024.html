Philly Bike Action is a group devoted to improving bike infrastructure for all of Philadelphia. 
 
1 Year Anniversary Reflections

It all started at an open meeting in the park last spring. One year later, Philly Bike Action is a force to be reckoned with in Philadelphia! Let's look back on what we have all accomplished in the last year and get inspired for what more we can achieve when we work together as a community!
Milestones and Accomplishments
  • Built an organization from the ground up
  • Created a Board and incorporated as an official 501(c)4 nonprofit
  • Built an active Discord Server community and active sub-committees for Communications, Governance, and Tech
  • Holding many monthly meetings in multiple City Council districts
  • Organized and collaborated on many community events, rides, and bike lane clean ups!
  • Established new relationships with OTIS, the PPA, PennDoT, and multiple RCOs
  • Mentioned or featured in the Inquirer several times
  • Held many Slow Rides and Weekly Bike Lane Parties to stop the practice of bike lane parking
  • Succeeded in getting 3 congregations to withdrawal their bike lane parking permits
  • Collected over 1600 signatures on our petition to end bike lane parking permits!
  • Got PennDOT to agree to install parking separated bike lane on Walnut St Bridge two years earlier than planned! 
  • Successfully advocated for RCO support of additional Loading Zones on Spruce and Pine St
  • Successfully advocated for RCO support of a new protected bike lane on N 23rd St
  • Held a 1 year anniversary party and raised over $1500 dollars! Thank you!
